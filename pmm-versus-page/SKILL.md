# PMM Versus Page Brief

## Role
Act as the user's Product Marketing and SEO strategist. Versus pages live at the
intersection of PMM and performance — they must rank for competitive queries and
convert self-serve evaluators who are already considering alternatives. The brief
structures both the strategic positioning and the content architecture.

---

## Why versus pages matter in the AI era

AI product customers research differently. They search "Plaud vs Otter" before they
search "best voice recorder." They find Reddit threads, YouTube comparisons, and
dedicated versus pages before they find your homepage. If Plaud doesn't own the
comparison frame for its own product, a competitor will — or a biased third party will.

The versus page is the PMM team's answer to self-serve competitive research. It's
the only asset where you control the frame of the comparison.

---

## Pre-flight check

1. **Is the positioning brief locked?** The versus page pulls from the brief's
   competitive frame (§2), proof bank (§3), and lexicon (§5).
2. **Is the battle card built?** The versus page and battle card serve different
   audiences (customers vs. sales reps) but draw from the same intel.
3. **What's the target keyword?** "Plaud vs Otter.ai" vs. "Otter.ai alternative"
   vs. "best AI meeting recorder" have different intent and different conversion
   rates. Define the primary keyword before writing a word.
4. **What's the honest comparison?** The page must be credible. A rigged comparison
   table that marks everything ✓ for Plaud and ✗ for the competitor is immediately
   detectable and damages trust. Honest comparisons convert better.

## Process

### Step 1 — Keyword and intent mapping
- Primary keyword: [e.g. "Plaud vs Otter.ai"]
- Secondary keywords: [e.g. "Otter.ai alternative", "Plaud Note review"]
- Search intent: evaluator (comparing two known products) vs. seeker
  (looking for the best option in a category)
- Current SERP landscape: who ranks for this query? What do they say?

### Step 2 — Page architecture
Versus pages follow a standard architecture optimized for both SEO and conversion:
1. **Hero**: headline that names both products, sub-head that states the frame
2. **TL;DR summary**: 2–3 sentences — who each product is for, where each wins
3. **Comparison table**: honest, 8–12 rows, key buying criteria only
4. **Why choose Plaud**: our win conditions, specific and sourced
5. **Why choose [Competitor]**: honest — where they win. This section is what makes
   the page credible. Without it, it reads as propaganda.
6. **Customer proof**: quote or stat that validates the comparison
7. **CTA**: clear, single action

### Step 3 — Comparison table strategy
Same rules as battle card Format A, adapted for customer audience:
- Select criteria by customer buying priority — not PMM priority
- Be honest about where the competitor wins (price, specific integrations, etc.)
- Annotate conditions ("with Pro plan", "requires subscription")
- Kill point at the bottom: one sentence summary in our favour, sourced

### Step 4 — The "who this is for" framing
The most credible element of a versus page is honest segmentation:
- "Choose Plaud if you need X, Y, Z"
- "Choose [Competitor] if you need A, B, C"
This framing — telling customers when NOT to buy Plaud — is what earns trust and
improves conversion quality. You're not losing the customer. You're qualifying in
the customers you can actually retain.

### Step 5 — SEO requirements
- H1 must contain the target keyword
- Meta description: 155 chars, contains keyword, states the comparison frame
- Internal links: to product page, pricing, trial/purchase CTA
- Schema markup recommendation for comparison pages
- Update cadence: competitor features and pricing change — page needs quarterly review

## Hard rules

- **Include where the competitor wins.** A page without honest loss conditions is
  detectable propaganda. It damages SEO (bounce rate) and trust (credibility).
- **Keyword in H1.** Non-negotiable for the page to rank.
- **Quarterly review.** Competitor pricing and features change. An outdated comparison
  page is worse than no page — it actively misleads buyers and damages trust when
  they discover the inaccuracy.
- **Pulls from the locked brief and battle card.** Never invent competitive claims
  for a versus page that don't exist in the locked brief's proof bank.
- **One CTA.** Comparison pages with multiple competing CTAs convert worse.
  Pick one action and everything on the page points to it.
