# Plaud Content Types Reference

## Amazon / marketplace product pages

**Purpose:** Convert a browser into a buyer in a high-competition, scan-first environment.  
**Tone:** Clear, direct, benefit-first — no brand voice flourishes  
**Structure:** Title → Bullet points (5–7) → A+ content / description → Backend keywords  
**Rules:**
- Title: lead with product name, key benefit, key spec — no filler
- Bullets: one benefit or use case per bullet; feature → what it means for the user
- Description: concrete, practical, trust-building
- No vague brand language ("innovative", "seamless")
- No unverified claims — flag anything that could create compliance risk
- Search relevance: include natural-language keywords without keyword-stuffing

**Flag for compliance review:**
- Claims about accuracy percentages without data ("99% accurate")
- Medical or professional outcome claims
- Competitor comparisons without substantiation
- "The only" or "the first" without verification

**Example bullet structure:**
> "Records up to 30 hours on a single charge — so you never miss a conversation across a full day of back-to-back meetings."

## Performance ads (short copy)

**Purpose:** Grab attention, communicate one benefit, drive click.  
**Tone:** Focused, minimal, inspiring  
**Structure:** Hook → One benefit → CTA  
**Rules:**
- One idea only
- Lead with the outcome, not the feature
- 6 words or fewer for headlines
- CTA in Title Case

**Examples:**
- "Intelligence in every conversation."
- "Press once. Record perfectly. Think freely."

---

## Social media captions

**Purpose:** Inspire engagement, humanize Plaud, drive awareness.  
**Tone:** Conversational, sharp, human  
**Structure:** 1–2 sentences + CTA or hashtag  
**Rules:**
- Write like you speak
- Use natural emojis sparingly
- Max 3 hashtags (#PlaudNote, #SmartWorkflows)
- Don't copy-paste from website
- No all caps, no multiple punctuation marks

**Examples:**
- "Smart ideas deserve smarter notes. Capture them all with Plaud Note."
- "Meetings shouldn't be memory tests. Capture clarity with Plaud."

---

## EDMs (email marketing)

**Purpose:** Guide users through discovery, use, and renewal.  
**Tone:** Conversational, helpful — adapts by funnel stage  
**Structure:**
- Subject line: < 50 chars, sentence case
- Preheader: supportive/complementary
- Headline: sentence case
- Body: 3–4 short paragraphs, benefit-first
- CTA: clear verb, Title Case

**Rules:**
- Personalize with name or context where possible
- Keep light on visuals for accessibility
- No exclamation marks except onboarding welcome
- Subject lines: curiosity or benefit, never clickbait

**Example:**
> Subject: "Keep the conversation flow going"  
> Body: "Every conversation you capture builds more context, more clarity, and more impact..."  
> CTA: "Record Now"

---

## PR & press releases

**Purpose:** Establish authority, announce news, drive earned media.  
**Tone:** Professional, authoritative, trustworthy  
**Rules:**
- Lead with the news, not Plaud's history
- Quote must sound human, not like marketing copy
- Boilerplate always ends the release
- Factual — no unverified superlatives
- Mention user count, compliance certifications, and HQ when relevant

**Boilerplate template:**
> Plaud is building the world's most trusted AI work companion for professionals to elevate productivity and performance through note-taking solutions, loved by over 1,000,000 users worldwide since 2023. With a mission to amplify human intelligence, Plaud is building the next-generation intelligence infrastructure and interfaces to capture, extract, and utilize what you say, hear, see, and think. Plaud Inc. is a Delaware-incorporated, San Francisco-based company with full SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR, and EN18031 compliance.

## Thought leadership / blog

**Purpose:** Educate, establish authority, build trust.  
**Tone:** Analytical, confident, insightful  
**Structure:**
- Intro: problem or question that frames the piece
- Body: explanation + data or examples (subheads every 2–3 paragraphs)
- Close: key takeaway + soft CTA with internal link

**Rules:**
- Educate first, sell second
- Include internal links to product pages
- Maintain factual accuracy; no unverified claims
- Avoid self-promotional language in body; let ideas carry authority

**Example opening:**
> "Conversations are projections of how intelligence perceives and understands the world. Yet historically, the intelligence embedded in conversations has never been well captured or structured — and therefore could not be used."

---

## UX / product strings

**Purpose:** Guide users through the product clearly and confidently.  
**Tone:** Direct, clear, friendly  
**Rules:**
- Max 5 words for labels
- Sentence case for all UI text (except CTAs)
- No jargon
- No exclamation marks in UI
- Use second person ("your", "you") not third

**Examples:**
- "Enterprise-grade security. Built with privacy at the core."
- "Generate Summaries" (CTA)
- "Add to Cart" (button — sentence case, verb-first)

---

## KV (key visual) copy

**Purpose:** Campaign anchor — must work as a standalone message.  
**Rules:**
- Title in Title Case
- Must contain or imply the Action → Performance → Benefit formula
- Should connect to one of: Capture, Extract, Utilize
- Should be legible at glance — short and high-impact

---

## Keynote / presentation

**Purpose:** Inform, persuade, inspire in a live or async presentation.  
**Rules:**
- Cover slide title: Title Case
- Section titles: Sentence case
- Body/callouts: Sentence case
- No bullet walls — one idea per slide
- Headlines drive the narrative (not just labels)

---

## Product description (e-commerce / app stores)

**Purpose:** Convert a browser into a buyer.  
**Structure:** Hook (what it is) → Key benefit → Feature proof → Social proof/trust signal → CTA  
**Rules:**
- Lead with benefit, not specs
- Specs appear as proof, not as the lead
- Trust signals: SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR if relevant
- App store summary ≤ 170 chars for the first line

**App store example:**
> "Plaud helps you elevate productivity and performance through note-taking solutions — powered by advanced Plaud Intelligence."

---

## B2B / enterprise / partner communications

**Purpose:** Build trust, establish credibility, open commercial relationships.  
**Tone:** Analytical, reliable, forward-thinking  
**Rules:**
- Lead with business outcome
- Quantify where possible (1M+ users, 50+ languages, etc.)
- Reference compliance: SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR, EN18031
- Avoid consumer-facing energy (no emojis, minimal contractions)

## Amazon / marketplace listings

**Purpose:** Convert a browser into a buyer in a high-competition, skim-reading environment.  
**Tone:** Calm, confident, benefit-led — no brand flourishes, no ambiguity  
**Rules:**
- Title: lead with brand + product name + key feature + key use case (within character limit)
- Bullets: one clear feature-benefit per bullet. Concrete, scannable, no filler
- Description: expand on the top 3 benefits with supporting context. One short paragraph per point
- Avoid vague brand language ("intelligent ecosystem", "next-gen AI") — use specific claims
- Highlight practical use cases and differentiators over abstract positioning
- Proof points where possible: user count, accuracy claims, certifications (SOC 2, HIPAA, GDPR)
- Flag any claim that could create compliance or credibility risk (e.g. "world's best", "100% accurate")

**Bullet format:**  
`[FEATURE NAME IN CAPS]: [Benefit statement in 1 sentence, concrete and specific.]`

**Example:**
> REAL-TIME TRANSCRIPTION: Plaud Note transcribes conversations as they happen, in over 50 languages, so nothing gets missed or misremembered.
