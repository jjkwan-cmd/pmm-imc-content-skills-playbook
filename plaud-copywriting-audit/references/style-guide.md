# Plaud English Writing Style Guide Reference

## Grammar rules

- **Voice:** Always active voice. ✅ "Plaud summarizes your meeting." ❌ "Your meeting is summarized by Plaud."
- **Sentence length:** 12–20 words. One clear idea per sentence.
- **POV:** Use "you" and "we" for direct engagement.
- **Filler words to cut:** very, really, just, actually
- **Contractions:** Use naturally (it's, we're, you'll). Don't over-formalize.
- **Redundancy:** Every word must earn its place. Delete words that change nothing.

## Punctuation rules

**Comma**
- Oxford comma before final "and/or": "what you say, hear, and think."
- After introductory phrases: "After recording, Plaud syncs automatically."
- Separate independent clauses with conjunctions: "Plaud records accurately, and Ask Plaud summarizes instantly."

**Em dash (—)**
- ⛔ Do NOT use in body copy, marketing, or UI text
- Only allowed in brand narrative / thought leadership for emphasis
- When tempted to use: rewrite as two sentences or use a comma

**En dash (–)**
- Use only for numeric or date ranges: "January–March 2025", "9 a.m.–5 p.m."

**Hyphen (-)**
- Join compound adjectives before nouns: "real-time summary", "AI-powered transcription"
- Do NOT hyphenate after adverbs ending in -ly: "highly accurate transcription" ✅

**Exclamation (!)**
- Almost never. Only in onboarding emails for a warm welcome. Never in product UI.

**Slash (/)**
- No spaces around: "Save/export", "600 min/mo", "$8.33 per member/month"

**Ellipsis (…)**
- Only for genuine omission or natural pause. Not for style.

**Quotation marks**
- Double quotes. Punctuation inside: "Ask Plaud finds what you need."

## Number and unit formatting

**General**
- Always numerals (not words): 10 g, 5 cm, 256 GB
- Space between number and most units: "10 g", "5 cm", "60 km/h"
- **Exception — digital storage: NO space:** "256GB", "12MB", "1TB"
- Never pluralize abbreviations: "10 min" not "10 mins"
- Percentages: NO space: "25%", not "25 %"
- Currency: NO space between symbol and number: "$100", "€1,200"

**Time**
- "9 a.m." / "3:30 p.m." — lowercase, space before
- Dates (EN-US): October 29, 2025
- Date ranges: en dash, no spaces: January–March 2025

---

## Acronyms & abbreviations

- Spell out on first use, then use acronym: Over the Air (OTA)
- Universally known acronyms (AI, GDPR, API) need no full form
- Never invent short forms for product names
- Uppercase, no periods: API not A.P.I.
- Plural: add "s", no apostrophe: APIs, FAQs
- Article based on pronunciation: "a URL", "an FAQ"

## Localization (EN-US default)

| Aspect | EN-US | EN-UK | EN-EU |
|---|---|---|---|
| Spelling | color, organize, center | colour, organise, centre | often accepts both |
| Date format | October 29, 2025 | 29 October 2025 | 29 Oct 2025 |
| Quotation marks | "double" | 'single' | varies |
| Units | Imperial where relevant | Metric | Metric |
| Currency | $1,234.56 | £1,234.56 | €1,234.56 |

**Always:** Avoid idioms or humor that may not translate culturally.
